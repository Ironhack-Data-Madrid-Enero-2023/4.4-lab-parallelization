id="single-post" class="archive-post eleven columns">\n<div class="nine columns offset-by-two alpha">\n<h1>\nRise of the Data Scientist </h1>\n</div>\n<div class="clr"></div>\n<div class="byinfo two columns alpha">\nJune 4, 2009\n<div style="margin-top:1.5rem">\n<h3 class="toplevel">Topic</h3>\n<strong><a href="https://flowingdata.com/category/design/" rel="category tag">Design</a>, <a href="https://flowingdata.com/category/statistics/" rel="category tag">Statistics</a></strong> </div>\n</div>\n<div class="nine columns omega">\n\n<div class="entry">\n<div class=\'img-right\'><img data-attachment-id="1591" data-permalink="https://flowingdata.com/2009/06/04/rise-of-the-data-scientist/butterfly_m/" data-orig-file="https://flowingdata.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/06/butterfly_m.jpg" data-orig-size="240,160" data-comments-opened="0" data-image-meta="{&quot;aperture&quot;:&quot;0&quot;,&quot;credit&quot;:&quot;&quot;,&quot;camera&quot;:&quot;&quot;,&quot;caption&quot;:&quot;&quot;,&quot;created_timestamp&quot;:&quot;0&quot;,&quot;copyright&quot;:&quot;&quot;,&quot;focal_length&quot;:&quot;0&quot;,&quot;iso&quot;:&quot;0&quot;,&quot;shutter_speed&quot;:&quot;0&quot;,&quot;title&quot;:&quot;&quot;}" height="160" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1591" /><br />\n<em><small>Photo by <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/majamarko/">majamarko</a></small></em>\n</div>\n<p>As we&#8217;ve all read by now, Google&#8217;s chief economist Hal Varian <a href="https://flowingdata.com/2009/02/25/googles-chief-economist-hal-varian-on-statistics-and-data/">commented</a> in January that the next sexy job in the next 10 years would be statisticians. Obviously, I whole-heartedly <a href="http://www.stat.ucla.edu/~nyau/">agree</a>. Heck, I&#8217;d go a step further and say they&#8217;re sexy now &#8211; mentally <em>and</em> physically. </p>\n<p>However, if you went on to read the rest of Varian&#8217;s interview, you&#8217;d know that by <em>statisticians</em>, he actually meant it as a general title for someone who is able to extract information from large datasets and then present something of use to non-data experts.<br />\n<span id="more-1585"></span></p>\n<h3>Sexy Skills of Data Geeks</h3>\n<p>As a follow up to Varian&#8217;s now-popular quote among data fans, Michael Driscoll of Dataspora, discusses the <a href="http://dataspora.com/blog/sexy-data-geeks/">three sexy skills of data geeks</a>. I won&#8217;t rehash the post, but here are the three skills that Michael highlights:</p>\n<ol>\n<li>Statistics &#8211; traditional analysis you&#8217;re used to thinking about</li>\n<li>Data Munging &#8211; parsing, scraping, and formatting data</li>\n<li>Visualization &#8211; graphs, tools, etc.</li>\n</ol>\n<h3>Oh, but there&#8217;s more&#8230;</h3>\n<p>These skills actually fit tightly with Ben Fry&#8217;s dissertation on <a href="http://benfry.com/phd/">Computational Information Design</a> (2004). However, Fry takes it a step further and argues for an entirely new field that combines the skills and talents from often disjoint areas of expertise:</p>\n<div style="border-top:1px dotted #CCC; border-bottom:1px dotted #CCC; margin-bottom: 20px;padding:20px 0;"><img data-attachment-id="1589" data-permalink="https://flowingdata.com/2009/06/04/rise-of-the-data-scientist/all-fields/" data-orig-file="https://flowingdata.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/06/all-fields.png" data-orig-size="544,85" data-comments-opened="0" data-image-meta="{&quot;aperture&quot;:&quot;0&quot;,&quot;credit&quot;:&quot;&quot;,&quot;camera&quot;:&quot;&quot;,&quot;caption&quot;:&quot;&quot;,&quot;created_timestamp&quot;:&quot;0&quot;,&quot;copyright&quot;:&quot;&quot;,&quot;focal_length&quot;:&quot;0&quot;,&quot;iso&quot;:&quot;0&quot;,&quot;shutter_speed&quot;:&quot;0&quot;,&quot;title&quot;:&quot;&quot;}" data-image-title="all-fields" data-image-description="" data-image-caption="" data-medium-file="https://flowingdata.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/06/all-fields.png" data-large-file="https://flowingdata.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/06/all-fields.png" loading="lazy" width="544" height="85" src="https://flowingdata.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/06/all-fields.png" alt="" title="all-fields" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1589" srcset="https://flowingdata.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/06/all-fields.png 544w, https://flowingdata.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/06/all-fields-250x39.png 250w" sizes="(max-width: 544px) 100vw, 544px" /></div>\n<ol>\n<li><strong>Computer Science</strong> &#8211; acquire and parse data</li>\n<li><strong>Mathematics, Statistics, &#038; Data Mining</strong> &#8211; filter and mine</li>\n<li><strong>Graphic Design</strong> &#8211; represent and refine</li>\n<li><strong>Infovis and Human-Computer Interaction (HCI)</strong> &#8211; interaction</li>\n</ol>\n<p>And after two years of highlighting visualization on FlowingData, it seems collaborations between the fields are growing more common, but more importantly, computational information design edges closer to reality. We&#8217;re seeing <em>data scientists</em> &#8211; people who can do it all &#8211; emerge from the rest of the pack.</p>\n<h3>Advantages of the Data Scientist</h3>\n<p>Think about all the visualization stuff you&#8217;ve been most impressed with or the groups that always seem to put out the best work. Martin Wattenberg. Stamen Design. Jonathan Harris. Golan Levin. Sep Kamvar. Why is their work always of such high quality? Because they&#8217;re not just students of computer science, math, statistics, or graphic design. </p>\n<p>They have a combination of skills that not just makes independent work easier and quicker; it makes collaboration more exciting and opens up possibilities in what can be done. Oftentimes, visualization projects are disjoint processes and involve a lot of waiting. Maybe a statistician is waiting for data from a computer scientist; or a graphic designer is waiting for results from an analyst; or an HCI specialist is waiting for layouts from a graphic designer. </p>\n<p>Let&#8217;s say you have several data scientists working together though. There&#8217;s going to be less waiting and the communication gaps between the fields are tightened.</p>\n<p>How often have we seen a visualization tool that held an excellent concept and looked great on paper but lacked the touch of HCI, which made it hard to use and in turn no one gave it a chance? How many important (and interesting) analyses have we missed because certain ideas could not be communicated clearly? The data scientist can solve your troubles.</p>\n<h3>An Application</h3>\n<p>This need for data scientists is quite evident in business applications where educated decisions need to be made swiftly. A delayed decision could mean lost opportunity and profit. Terabytes of data are coming in whether it be from websites or from sales across the country, but in an area where Excel is the tool of choice (or force), there are limitations, hence all the tools, applications, and consultancies to help out. This of course applies to areas outside of business as well.</p>\n<h3>Learn and Prosper</h3>\n<p>Even if you&#8217;re not into visualization, you&#8217;re going to need at least a subset of the skills that Fry highlights if you want to seriously mess with data. Statisticians should know APIs, databases, and how to scrape data; designers should learn to do things programmatically; and computer scientists should know how to analyze and find meaning in data. </p>\n<p>Basically, the more you learn, the more you can do, and the higher in demand you will be as the amount of data grows and the more people want to make use of it.</p>\n<div class="clr"></div>\n</div>\n<div class="share"></div>\n<div class=\'yarpp yarpp-related yarpp-related-website yarpp-template-yarpp-template-list\'>\n<div id="related">\n<h3 class="toplevel">Related</h3>\n<ul>\n<li>\n<a href="https://flowingdata.com/2011/08/11/data-scientist-the-hottest-job-you-havent-heard-of/" rel="bookmark" title="Data Scientist: The hottest job you haven&#8217;t heard of">\nData Scientist: The hottest job you haven&#8217;t heard of</a>\n\n</li>\n<li>\n<a href="https://flowingdata.com/2012/09/19/hiring-a-data-scientist/" rel="bookmark" title="Hiring a data scientist">\nHiring a data scientist</a>\n\n</li>\n<li>\n<a href="https://flowingdata.com/2018/08/21/what-data-scientists-really-do/" rel="bookmark" title="What data scientists really do">\nWhat data scientists really do</a>\n\n</li>\n</ul>\n<div class="clear-line"></div>\n</div>\n</div>\n</div>\n<div class="clr"></div>\n<div class="nine columns offset-by-two alpha">\n<span id="comments"></span>\n<div id="comments-block">\n<h3 class="centered">47 Comments</h3>\n<ul class="commentlist">\n<li class="comment even thread-even depth-1" id="li-comment-30568">\n<div id="comment-30568" class="">\n<div class="comment-content">\n<div class="comment-author vcard">\n<cite class="fn"><a href=\'http://www.cloudera.com\' rel=\'external nofollow ugc\' class=\'url\'>jeff</a></cite> <span class="comment-meta">&mdash;
